Scuba Diving

I am a certified advanced scuba diver by the Professional Association of Diving Instructors (PADI), an international scuba diver training organization. I find that I particularly enjoy sports activities that require courage and self-confidence, and scuba diving is a good fit based on this personality of mine. It definitely motivates me to be more courageous. Not only that, but it also helps me motivate other first-time scuba divers in my team who need courage and confidence to try their first dive.

Why Do I Dive?

Integrate with others

Similar to music, sport is a great platform that promotes integration between people of different races. Scuba diving is an activity that connects myself with more people around the world because diving is a sport that normally requires buddies (for safety purposes). Everyone can also use PADI / SSI certificate to dive around the world.

Appreciate my role

Relaxing myself in the water and experiencing astonishing underwater creatures helps me appreciate nature and motivates me to do more to protect the environment. For this reason, I feel proud to be an engineering researcher because I have the chance to contribute in shaping a cleaner and more sustainable environment so that future generations can also experience this beautiful mother nature.

Promote mental health

Immersing myself completely in the ocean while enjoying marine life is definitely an effective way to relieve mental stress. Everyone needs to have good mental health regardless of their backgrounds and cultures. I believe having good mental health is essential to drive me doing my tasks and serving others more effectively.

Learn to accept criticism

Learning how to dive or having some fun dives allows me to meet different instructors or divemaster at different dive locations. Often I learn from them and seek their feedback on how I can further improve my scuba diving skills, and indirectly this sport has helped me to be more open in accepting criticism.
